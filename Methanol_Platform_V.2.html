Cost Model References & Methodology
① Turton et al. 2009 (Primary)
Turton R., Bailie R.C., Whiting W.B., Shaeiwitz J.A. — Analysis, Synthesis, and Design of Chemical Processes, 3rd ed., Prentice Hall, 2009.
Tables A.1–A.4. Cp° correlations for 30+ equipment types. CEPCI base: 397 (2001). FBM bare-module factors with Fm (material) & Fp (pressure) corrections.
② Seider et al. 2009 (Distillation & Vessels)
Seider W.D., Seader J.D., Lewin D.R., Widagdo S. — Product and Process Design Principles, 3rd ed., Wiley, 2009.
Chapter 16: purchased equipment cost data, tray columns, pressure vessel scaling. Used for C301/C302 column cross-validation.
③ Peters, Timmerhaus & West 2004
Peters M.S., Timmerhaus K.D., West R.E. — Plant Design and Economics for Chemical Engineers, 5th ed., McGraw-Hill, 2004.
Used for pump, compressor cost curves. Six-tenths rule exponents validated for K201–K204 compressor train.
④ NREL/TP-5100-57420, 2013
Dutta A., Talmadge M., Hensley J. et al. — Process Design and Economics for Biochemical Conversion of Lignocellulosic Biomass to Ethanol, NREL Technical Report, 2013.
Area-specific capital costs for syngas compression and methanol synthesis loop used for cross-check of K201 train and R201 reactor costs.
⑤ Ulrich & Vasudevan 2004
Ulrich G.D., Vasudevan P.T. — Chemical Engineering Process Design and Economics: A Practical Guide, 2nd ed., Process Publishing, 2004.
Heat exchanger and vessel bare-module cost charts. Used for E207/E208 intercooler & T351 condensate drum cross-reference. Chart 5-41 to 5-53.

Process Overview
Syngas enters V201 KO drum → compressed in three stages K201/K202/K203 with intercoolers → preheated via E209/E210 → reacted in R201A+B at 52 bar, 220–260 °C over Cu/ZnO/Al₂O₃ → effluent cooled through E301 → condensate to T351 → purified in columns C301 (light ends) and C302 (product). Vapour recycled via K204.

Sensitivity Analysis — GWP vs Electricity Carbon Intensity
Key Modelling Assumptions
Functional unit: 1 tonne (1000 kg) of Grade-AA methanol at plant gate, ≥99.9 wt% purity.
System boundary: Cradle-to-gate; excludes MeOH combustion, transport to customer, and plant decommissioning.
Syngas carbon intensity: 0.25 kg CO₂-eq/kg syngas (base case, natural gas-derived reformate). Literature range: 0.18–0.52 kg CO₂-eq/kg.
Catalyst production: Cu/ZnO/Al₂O₃ — 0.012 kg CO₂-eq/kg MeOH attributed (mass-based, lifetime 3 years). Source: Galán-Martín et al., 2021.
Water use: Process water from municipal supply. Cooling water in closed-loop (only makeup counted: 3.2 m³/t MeOH).
Steam credit: MPS export valued at natural gas boiler reference (LHV efficiency 90%, 0.198 kg CO₂-eq/kWh thermal, DE grid 2023).
Light ends purge: Combusted in plant flare. Resulting CO₂ (fossil) added to direct emissions inventory.
Plant lifetime: 20 years, 8000 hr/yr (consistent with TEA assumptions).
Literature Sources & Data Quality
Ott et al. (2012): Methanol; Ullmann's Encyclopedia. Baseline process GWP 0.74–0.95 kg CO₂-eq/kg MeOH for NG feedstock.
Galán-Martín et al. (2021): LCA of Power-to-Methanol. Chem. Eng. J. Catalyst and syngas upstream values.
IEA (2023): Net Zero by 2050. Reference electricity emission factors and green H₂ trajectories.
ecoinvent v3.9: Background system — electricity, water, transport, waste treatment. System model: cut-off.
EU JRC (2021): EF 3.1 characterisation factors. GWP100 using AR5 values (CO₂=1, CH₄=28, N₂O=265).
Uncertainty note: Background inventory: high confidence. Syngas CI: medium (±30%). Catalyst manufacturing: low (±50%). Overall GWP (95% CI): 0.82 ± 0.21 kg CO₂-eq/kg MeOH.

Decarbonisation Levers
Green electricity switch: Replacing grid power with offshore wind (0.025 kg CO₂/kWh) reduces GWP by ≈0.19 kg CO₂-eq/kg MeOH (−23%).
Green H₂ / renewable syngas: Switching to electrolytic H₂ + captured CO₂ reduces GWP to ≈0.12 kg CO₂-eq/kg MeOH (−85%).
CCS on purge gas: Capturing CO₂ from light ends purge combustion adds −0.06 kg CO₂-eq/kg MeOH credit.
